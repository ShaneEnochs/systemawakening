You feel... foggy.

The last thing you remember is reclining in your chair, half-watching a rerun of a late-night comedy show. You must have dozed off, so that means this has to be a dream.

But this is the strangest, most vivid dream you've ever had.

You sit cross-legged on a bitterly cold stone floor encircled by dozens of flickering candles, the chill seeping straight through to your bones. Beyond the dim perimeter of shifting light, you can just make out the dark shapes of... something. People, maybe, though it's impossible to tell. Your pulse thumps in your ears as your confusion compounds with each passing second.

"Did it work?" a woman's voice asks from somewhere to your right.

You automatically turn toward the sound. Darkness and shadow stretch and twist past the candle flames, refusing to reveal whoever is out there, but low murmurs rippling around you lets you know you are surrounded.

"Who are you?" a masculine voice booms from directly in front of you, though he too remains hidden in the gloom. "What is your name?"

The candles flare all at once, blazing bright enough to make you flinch, and then the darkness pulls back.

You are in a stone chamber deep beneath a keep. The walls are carved bedrock. The floor — where it isn't scoured ash — is covered in intricate arrays drawn in a substance that is either dried blood or something worse. Around the edge of the room, perhaps two dozen people stand in silence, most of them wearing the expressions of people who have just witnessed something they cannot quite believe.

Three people are closer than the rest.

The man who spoke is tall and broad-shouldered, with the kind of face that has been broken at least once and healed crooked. He is watching you with an intensity that is not quite hope and not quite grief — something held tightly in between.

To your right, the woman who first spoke. She is slight and dark-eyed and she is already watching you with the focused assessment of someone running calculations. She does not look relieved. She looks like she is waiting to see if something is going to go wrong.

Behind them both, standing near the chamber door, a younger woman holds a lantern and says nothing. She has the look of someone who knew the cost of tonight before it was paid.

And on the floor, between you and the three of them, there is a fine grey ash arranged in a rough circle.

Something about that ash makes the back of your throat tighten.

"My name is Hudson," the tall man says. "Commander of the Baron's forces." A pause. "Of Oakhaven's forces, now."

"I'm Stella," says the dark-eyed woman. "And there is a great deal I need to explain to you. But first —" she produces a small lantern of her own and crouches to your level, studying your face, "— I need to know if you are well enough to stand."
