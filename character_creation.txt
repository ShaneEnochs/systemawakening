The year is 1815. A letter has arrived — heavy paper, black wax seal, a solicitor's cramped hand. You have inherited an estate, a title, and all the trouble that comes with both.

But first, the solicitor requires certain details for the documentation.

*input name "What is your given name?"

*input family "And your family name?"

*page_break

The documents are prepared. The solicitor pauses, pen hovering over the final line.

"One last formality, if you please — for the formal record of peerage."

*choice
  #He / Him
    *set pronouns_subject "he"
    *set pronouns_object "him"
    *set pronouns_possessive "his"
    *set pronouns_possessive_pronoun "his"
    *set pronouns_reflexive "himself"
    *set pronouns_label "he/him"
    *set title "Baron"
  #She / Her
    *set pronouns_subject "she"
    *set pronouns_object "her"
    *set pronouns_possessive "her"
    *set pronouns_possessive_pronoun "hers"
    *set pronouns_reflexive "herself"
    *set pronouns_label "she/her"
    *set title "Baroness"
  #They / Them
    *set pronouns_subject "they"
    *set pronouns_object "them"
    *set pronouns_possessive "their"
    *set pronouns_possessive_pronoun "theirs"
    *set pronouns_reflexive "themself"
    *set pronouns_label "they/them"
    *set title "Baron/ess"

The solicitor nods, signs the final page, and hands you the deed.

"Congratulations, ${title} ${family}. The estate is yours."

{They} fold the letter carefully, tuck it into {their} coat, and step outside into the pale English morning. Somewhere to the north, a village waits for its new lord. Somewhere beyond that, the future — whatever ${name} chooses to make of it.
