It's a particular variety of vertigo, but not the kind that comes from heights or spinning or falling. A singularity.

*page_break Four months ago.

Four months ago, the portal mages had closed the last entrance that connected the surface to the city of Solace. The city was built without any traditional methods of accessing it, so there were no gates or tunnels; just a mile of solid rock between the final sanctuary of humans and the horrors above.

Solace was the last coalition of surviving nations, built into the earth's hollow because there was nowhere else to go but down. Legions of geomancers carved through the rock, artificers and architecture mages laid the streets and buildings, and glamourers used potent illusions created a false sky and sun on the ceiling of the cavern that was always in the position of high noon, though it brightened and dimmed to the luminosity of moonlight depending on the time of day. All of this was done so the survivors could look up at something other than stone in despair. 

Unfortunately, despair was an old friend of everyone here, so it was felt, regardless. How could anything else be expected? The world was overrun with literal monsters, and the only place left to hide from them was in a cave. 

It was such an ironic turn of fate that the civilization of mankind began in caves and it would end in one as well.

*page_break But the plan mostly worked, for all of four months.

After the portals were closed, as far as anyone knows, there wasn't a single living human being left on the surface. The monsters, predictably, had done what monsters do when left entirely to their own devices: autonomously summoned more of their kind, fought, and consumed each other to grow more powerful and eventually evolved. They got smarter, and they eventually began looking for wherever humanity had tried to hide.

It didn't take long for them to start looking down.

But in two hours and ten minutes, none of it will matter anymore. You find, to your astonishment, that you almost manage to convince yourself into believing it.

Almost...
