*title [Prologue] Induction

Some people are great at life. Standing in line at the Exxon with your Monster and Slim Jim, you realize you might be great at death. After all, when the robber screaming at you to get on the floor inevitably pulls the trigger, it will be your third time dying. You idly wonder if you'll wake up in a new world yet again, or if this death will be permanent.

You like a lot of things about this world better than the last two. Netflix is great, and you just started a new wildlife documentary narrated by David Attenborough. It's about baby dinosaurs, which neither of your previous worlds had. Cute little things. The parents looked terrifying though, so it’s probably for the best they’re extinct.

Energy drinks are a huge plus. Your first world had alchemical equivalents, but they tasted like battery acid smells.

The best part, though? No dungeons and no monsters. Also, almost no one carries a sword here unless they’re a weirdo, and even then, those are flimsy, dull replicas. Nothing like what you're used to.

Cars are fine, but your second world had magi-tech vehicles that ran clean for a decade on a single mana sphere. Fuel prices here are a joke, but at least you can walk the city.

Sure, there are dangers. That first night waking up in Central Park, you almost got hit by a city bus. And then there are desperate people doing desperate, dangerous things. But you suspect they exist in every world.

*page_break

"I said get on the ground," yells the increasingly frantic masked man, pointing the gun at your nose as you tear open your Slim Jim and take a bite.

The food here is by far the best, though. As your teeth snap through the salty stick of processed meat, you've never been more sure of anything. Culinary perfection.

But you're tired. Across three lives, you’ve lived 142 years. This is the shortest run at only nine years, but it's been enough. There's no essence or mana in this world, so you feel weak and dull in comparison to your past lives. Either way, you really hope it's the last.

[i]BANG![/i]

*page_break

The shot is deafening, but you don't actually feel anything. For a second, you wonder if this dweeb somehow missed from three feet away. But then the second passes and the pain hits your upper arm, feeling like a hot coal pressed against your flesh. You look at the blood soaking your sleeve.

"Seriously?" you ask, incredulously. Through the holes in his mask, you see his eyes are wide with shock. This is probably the first time he's ever hurt anyone. He's clearly terrified, unsure of what to do now. He stumbles backward into the counter candy display, sending a cascade of colorful wrappers across the floor. He slips on them.

The gun jerks in his hand, and his two accidental shots are somehow far more deadly accurate than his intentional one.

The first bullet slams into your gut. It hurts infinitely worse than the arm, but luckily, the agony doesn't last long as you catch the next round in your neck.

Well, crap. You're still here. The floating box is new, though, even if it is pretty redundant.

You were there. You know you died.

You wonder for likely the hundredth time if you keep getting reincarnated because some god or goddess has a sick sense of humor. Your first world had thousands of deities, so it's possible one of them is doing this to you. But can they reach across realities? You weren't exactly religious, but you had never heard of such a thing during that life.

*page_break

Your second life didn't appear to have gods at all. They had lots of demons, though. The infernal creatures constantly poured through portals trying to wipe the residents out.

And then there was Earth. It seemed like a lot of people were monotheistic, though a few clung to oldder pantheons, and a massive chunk didn't believe in anything supernatural at all. That made perfect sense, considering the place lacked even a single drop of essence or mana.

Taking in your surroundings, you're relieved to find yourself sitting in a quiet grassy field. You still haven't figured out the mechanics of this process, unsure whether you hijack a local's body or just pop into existence looking like the natives. No one has ever said they recognize you, so you suspect it's the second one.

Holding up your hands, they look identical to the ones you just left on Earth: five fingers, soft skin, short fingernails. A quick pat-down confirms the rest: one torso, two legs, two feet. Looks like you're human here, too.
